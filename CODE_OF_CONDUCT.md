# Code of Conduct

PocketClaw aims to keep collaboration calm, useful, and low-drama.

## Expected behavior

When participating in issues, pull requests, reviews, or other repository discussions:

- be respectful and specific
- assume good faith, but stay honest about problems
- focus on facts, code, docs, and observable behavior
- keep criticism constructive and tied to the work
- prefer small, clear suggestions over aggressive pile-ons
- respect project scope and stated technical boundaries

## Unacceptable behavior

This repository does not welcome behavior such as:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- insults, hostility, or repeated bad-faith engagement
- doxxing or sharing private information without permission
- posting secrets, credentials, tokens, or other sensitive material
- spam, trolling, or deliberately derailing technical discussion
- pressure to expand scope through manipulation rather than technical reasoning

## Collaboration norms for PocketClaw

Because PocketClaw is intentionally narrow in scope, contributors should also avoid:

- turning routine discussions into broad architecture fights
- repeatedly pushing out-of-scope backend or protocol inventions without a concrete fit
- mixing unrelated cleanup into focused maintenance work

## Maintainer actions

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that undermine a healthy working environment.
They may also close discussions that are out of scope, repeatedly hostile, or no longer constructive.

## Reporting

If something crosses the line, open a repository issue only if that can be done safely and without posting sensitive details.
For security-sensitive problems, follow [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md).

If the repository later adds a dedicated moderation or private contact path, use that for non-public reports.
